Mental Wellbeing

By Nataly Camacho

People with depression can minimize their episodes reaching out to loved ones or mental health professionals

Research

Hypothesis

People with depression can minimize their episodes reaching out to loved ones or mental health professionals

From the Interviews

7 out of 7 interviewees practice active hobbies

37 out of 70 persons presented signs related with depression

42.9% of the people just waited for the depressive episodes to pass

82.9% of the people didn’t seek professional help, even while being aware of their depressive episodes

What The Interviewees Said

“I tracked my depressive episodes and it helped me realize I needed professional help”
”I tried to keep a diary with my mood changes, but I would just forget it everytime”
”When I start to feel bad I don't know what to do”
“I don't feel comfortable reaching out to a mental health professional”
People don’t seek professional help because they don’t realize how big their problem really is

Insights

In order to organice the findings from the interviews an affinity diagram was created

The diagram was divided by:
  • What people understood about depression
  • Awareness — understanding — acting on it
  • How they acted on it
  • Depression is a topic people prefer to deal on it’s own
  • Causes
  • Interviewees mentioned common causes they felt lead to their depression
People don't reach out when they are feeling depressed. They tend to isolate and avoid doing the activities they normally like

Pain Points

After analyzing what the interviewees said these were the most notorious pain points

  • Depressed people won’t go to a mental wellbeing professional
  • People don’t know what triggers their depression
  • People are not comfortable expressing the state of their mental wellbeing
  • People don’t track their depression. So they can’t be prepared if there is some sort of pattern
  • People don’t have enough information on their depression to take actions to get better

Problem Statement

Depressed people need a way to track their depressive episodes so they can understand why they happen and act to get better

Prototyping

Paper Prototypes, Crazy 8's and non stop user testing helped get the most intuitive interaction

As I was sketching I decided that since depression is such an emotional, personal and very delicate topic, it would be good for the application to have a human touch. For this I added an option to contact for help, from a friend or from a suicide hotline, if the user was to deep into their depressive episode. I thought about this when I first started doing the interviews.

I thought users would feel more comfortable talking about their depression anonymously online or with a mental health professional. This turned out to be such an incorrect assumption because all the interviewees preferred to either bottle their emotions up or share them with a close friend. Since not all users had someone to reach out I left the suicide hotline link in case of an emergency
